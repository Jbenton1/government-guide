The Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (SDVOSB) certification gives qualifying veteran-owned businesses preferential access to federal contracting opportunities. The federal government is required to award at least 5% of all contracting dollars, over $31 billion annually, to certified SDVOSBs. This goal was increased from 3% to 5% by the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2024 (P.L. 118-31).

To qualify, your business must be at least 51% owned and controlled by one or more service-disabled veterans, with the veteran(s) managing day-to-day operations and making long-term decisions. Any VA disability rating percentage qualifies.

The Small Business Administration (SBA) manages the certification process through its VetCert program, which took over from the Department of Veterans Affairs in January 2023. Self-certification is no longer accepted. You must be officially certified through SBA VetCert to compete for SDVOSB set-aside contracts or count toward agency contracting goals. The certification is free, takes approximately 60-90 days to process, and remains valid for three years.

Before applying to VetCert, you'll need to complete your SAM.gov registration (the federal government's System for Award Management).
