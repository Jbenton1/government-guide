The Concept

Why "Colors" of Money?

In the private sector, budget dollars move freely between categories. Federal appropriations don't work that way.

Congress doesn't hand the Department of Defense one big checkbook. Instead, they create separate, legally-restricted funding streams, each with its own rules for what it can buy, how long it's available, and how much can be spent.

The term "colors of money" comes from this restriction. You can't paint a MILCON project with O&M dollars any more than you can pay for paint with Monopoly money. Wrong color = violation of federal law.

Three Unbreakable Rules

Every federal dollar is bound by three legal constraints. Violating any of them triggers the Antideficiency Act, with consequences ranging from administrative action to criminal penalties.

Why This Matters For Your Project

Understanding which color funds your project tells you:

  • Timeline expectations: A MILCON project takes 5-7 years from requirement to completion. An O&M service contract can move in months.
  • Who controls the money: Different appropriations flow through different program managers and contracting offices.
  • When to engage: Specifications for MILCON projects are written during the design phase, funded 1-2 years before construction dollars arrive.
  • What's at risk: Under a Continuing Resolution, new MILCON projects can't start. Understanding the budget cycle helps you anticipate delays.

Reference Guide

Appropriation Deep Dive

Detailed information on each appropriation category and its applications.

Research, Development, Test & Evaluation

Obligation Period: 2 Years
FY25 Request: $143.2 Billion (17%)

Overview

RDT&E funds the development of new capabilities, from basic scientific research through system testing. These funds support contractors and government laboratories working to develop equipment, materials, and software for defense applications. Unlike Procurement, RDT&E allows incremental funding.

Budget Activities

  • 6.1 Basic Research: Fundamental scientific study without specific applications
  • 6.2 Applied Research: Systematic study to develop useful materials, devices, or methods
  • 6.3 Advanced Technology Development: Prototype demonstrations and technology maturation
  • 6.4 Advanced Component Development & Prototypes: Evaluating integrated technologies
  • 6.5 System Development & Demonstration: Full-scale development for operational use
  • 6.6 RDT&E Management Support: Test ranges, labs, supporting infrastructure
  • 6.7 Operational System Development: Upgrades to fielded systems
  • 6.8 Software & Digital Technology Pilot Programs: Agile software initiatives

Key Note

Science & Technology (S&T) refers specifically to Budget Activities 6.1-6.3, representing the "seed corn" for future defense capabilities. This ~$21.5B portfolio funds breakthrough research at universities, labs, and contractors.

Procurement

Obligation Period: 3 Years (5 for Ships)
FY25 Request: $167.5 Billion (20%)

Overview

Procurement funds investment items: equipment approved for production that will be used operationally. This includes weapons systems, vehicles, aircraft, ships, ammunition, and other end items exceeding $250,000 unit cost. Initial spares and production-related services are also funded through Procurement.

Key Characteristics

  • Full Funding Required: The entire cost must be appropriated upfront before contract award
  • Service-Specific Accounts: Aircraft Procurement Air Force, Missile Procurement Army, Shipbuilding and Conversion Navy, etc.
  • Extended Period for Ships: Navy Shipbuilding has a 5-year obligation period due to long construction timelines
  • Unit Cost Threshold: Items over $250,000 per unit typically require Procurement vs. O&M

Operations & Maintenance

Obligation Period: 1 Year
FY25 Request: $337.9 Billion (40%)

Overview

O&M is the largest DoD appropriation, funding day-to-day operations and expenses. This covers civilian salaries, travel, training, base operations, depot maintenance, spare parts, and minor construction under $1 million.

FSRM Component

Facilities Sustainment, Restoration & Modernization (FSRM) is a critical subcomponent of O&M. FSRM funds maintain existing facilities through regular maintenance, major repairs, and component replacements. FY2024 NDAA Section 2841 established new requirements for services to invest FSRM at increasing percentages of total Plant Replacement Value, reaching 4% by FY2030.

O&M vs. MILCON Boundary

  • Under $1 million: Minor construction can use O&M funds
  • $1M - $9 million: Falls into Unspecified Minor MILCON
  • Over $9 million: Requires full MILCON authorization and appropriation

Military Construction

Obligation Period: 5 Years
FY25 Budget: $17.5 Billion (2%)

Overview

MILCON funds major construction projects: bases, facilities, schools, hospitals, and infrastructure. Unlike other appropriations which are part of the Defense Appropriations Act, MILCON is enacted separately through the Military Construction and Veterans Affairs Appropriations Act.

Approval Thresholds (FY2024 NDAA)

  • Under $750,000: Can proceed without Secretary approval
  • $750,000 - $4 million: Requires Secretary approval (Unspecified Minor MILCON)
  • Over $4 million: Requires Secretary approval plus Congressional notification
  • Over $9 million: Requires specific Congressional authorization and appropriation
  • Indo-Pacific Command: Special authority allows projects up to $30 million

Timeline Reality

Major MILCON projects typically take 5-7 years from requirements identification through construction completion. This includes requirements development, programming, Congressional authorization, appropriation, design, and construction phases. Design work (P&D) typically occurs 12-24 months before construction funding.

Military Personnel

Obligation Period: 1 Year
FY25 Request: $181.9 Billion (21%)

Overview

MILPERS funds military pay, allowances, bonuses, and personnel-related costs. This includes permanent change of station moves, subsistence, temporary lodging, and retired pay accrual.

Contractor Relevance

While MILPERS rarely directly impacts contractor work, understanding its 21% share of the DoD budget provides context for overall resource constraints. Personnel costs are relatively fixed and grow with end strength and pay raises, which can squeeze other accounts.

USACE Civil Works

Appropriation Act: Energy & Water Development
FY24/25 Budget: $8.7 Billion

Overview

USACE Civil Works operates under a separate appropriations structure from DoD military activities. Civil Works is funded through the Energy and Water Development Appropriations Act, not the Defense Appropriations Act.

The Five Civil Works Accounts

  • Investigations: Funds feasibility studies for new water resource projects
  • Construction: Funds design and construction of authorized projects
  • Operation & Maintenance: The largest account. Covers ongoing operation of locks, dams, channels, harbors
  • Mississippi River & Tributaries (MR&T): Dedicated account for the Mississippi system
  • FUSRAP: Environmental cleanup of early atomic weapons sites

Authorization-Appropriation Split

USACE projects require both authorization (via WRDA, typically biennial) and appropriation. That's a two-step process. A project can be authorized but never funded, or funding can be delayed years after authorization. This creates planning uncertainty for A&E firms and contractors.

Budget Cycle

The PPBE Process

How DoD translates strategy into funded programs through the annual budget cycle.

Jan - Mar

Planning Phase

USD Policy produces Defense Planning Guidance (DPG), translating National Defense Strategy into force development priorities.

Apr - Jul

Programming Phase

Services develop Program Objective Memoranda (POMs). CAPE reviews and updates the 5-year FYDP.

Aug - Dec

Budgeting Phase

Services submit Budget Estimate Submissions. Comptroller reviews. Final budget goes to OMB in December.

February

President's Budget

Budget submitted to Congress. The public first sees DoD's funding priorities for the upcoming fiscal year.

Mar - Sep

Congressional Action

Committees hold hearings, mark up bills. Authorization (NDAA) and Appropriations acts developed separately.

October 1

New Fiscal Year

FY begins. If appropriations not enacted, Continuing Resolution (CR) provides temporary funding at prior-year levels.

Ongoing

Execution Phase

Programs execute. Performance evaluated against plans. Results inform future planning cycles.
